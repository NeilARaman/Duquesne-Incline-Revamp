Accessibility Information

  • The Upper Station located at 1220 Grandview Ave is wheelchair accessible.
  • The observation deck is wheelchair accessible.
  • The historic incline cars can accommodate wheelchairs up to 25 inches in width.
  • There are many steps to traverse from the lower station parking lot.

Experience the Ride

Important Visitor Information

Payment

Exact cash fare upon arrival. Cash fares and Port Authority connect cards accepted. No debit or credit cards.

Facilities

Sorry, no restrooms. The Duquesne Incline has been part of Pittsburgh's transportation system since 1877 like a trolley or the cable cars of San Francisco.

Group Visits

School groups and bus tours are welcome. Educational visits have no extra charge for students and youth groups.

Photography

Photography is permitted and encouraged! The observation deck offers one of the best views of Pittsburgh's skyline.

What to Expect

The incline ride takes approximately 2-3 minutes. At the Upper Station, enjoy the observation deck with panoramic views of Pittsburgh's three rivers and downtown skyline. Historical photographs and mechanical displays are available for viewing.

Souvenirs

Visit our gift shop at the Upper Station to purchase Duquesne Incline souvenirs, postcards, books about Pittsburgh history, and locally made items. All proceeds support the preservation of the historic incline.
